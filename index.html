Nadine Jacobs

In January 2025, I completed my Master of Science in Health Science and Technology from ETH Zurich. My background in medical technology has equipped me with practical skills in clinical research, health data analysis, and the application of machine learning in real-world healthcare settings.

I’m passionate about the intersection of medicine and machine learning, with a particular focus on leveraging data-driven insights to enhance clinical decision-making and improve patient outcomes.

Selection of Work

Association between Knee Osteoarthritis Progression and Acoustic Emissions

Master Thesis, Lower Extremities Group, Schulthess Klinik 2024

I investigated the progression of knee osteoarthritis in a clinical study using acoustic emissions and machine learning. By analyzing joint sound patterns, I identified distinct audio biomarkers associated with disease development, offering a potential non- invasive approach to monitoring osteoarthritis progression.

Personalized bone organoids for drug efficacy testing in children with osteogenesis imperfecta

Research Internship, Lab for Bone Biomechanics, ETH Zurich, 2023

During a three-month research internship, I independently designed and conducted a study investigating the effects of anabolic bone drugs using 3D bioprinted bone organoids. I maintained bone organoid cultures, performed biomechanical and imaging analyses (micro-CT), and presented my findings through a comprehensive report and final presentation.
